    • our history

    Emerging from the vibrant yet often misconstrued depths of Kibera, Kenya's largest urban slum,
    Tembea Kibera stands as a testament to the indomitable spirit of community and the transformative
    power of collective action. Founded in 2016 by a group of passionate and dedicated individuals,
    all hailing from the very streets of Kibera, Tembea Kibera embarked on a mission to redefine the
    narrative surrounding their community, one step at a time.

    But Tembea Kibera's impact extended far beyond guided tours. Recognizing the tangible needs
    of the community, the organization actively engaged in a multitude of social improvement
    projects, each designed to uplift and empower the lives of Kibera residents. Trash clean-up initiatives
    restored a sense of cleanliness and pride to the community, while sanitary pad giveaways addressed
    a critical need and promoted women's health.

    With each tour, Tembea Kibera meticulously wove together visits to inspiring landmarks like the
    women's empowerment project, the Kibera Girls Soccer Academy, and the Kibera Town Center,
    each site a testament to the community's unwavering determination to progress. Along the way,
    participants were introduced to the Victoria Bone Group, a collective of artisans crafting exquisite
    jewelry from repurposed bones, and other locally-owned businesses, each testament to Kibera's
    entrepreneurial spirit.

    The organization's action plan, a harmonious blend of intangible and tangible goals, stood as a
    testament to its holistic approach to community development. While the guided tours aimed to
    reshape perceptions and reclaim Kibera's narrative, the tangible projects provided much-needed
    support and empowerment to the community's residents.
